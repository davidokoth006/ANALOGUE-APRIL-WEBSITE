Our Movement

Analogue April is a grassroots initiative challenging the pervasive culture of constant connectivity. Each April, we encourage individuals to swap their smartphones for basic handsets, take a break from social media, and reconnect with the world around them.

Our Cause

We advocate for delaying smartphone and social media use in children, promoting digital mindfulness, and fostering real-world connections.

Our Impact

Participants report improved sleep, heightened focus, and deeper personal connections. Families share stories of strengthened relationships.

Our Story

Born from a conversation between friends overwhelmed by smartphone use, Analogue April has grown into a national movement.

Digital Challenges

Try these challenges to gradually reduce your screen time and prepare for Analogue April.

View All Challenges

The Commuter Challenge

Leave your phone in your bag for your entire commute—no scrolling, no music, no messages. Just be present with your thoughts or the world around you.

The Door Drop

As soon as you arrive home, drop your phone in a bowl by the door. Enjoy an evening phone-free—cook, talk, play, read, or unwind without notifications.

The Bedroom Ban

Keep your phone out of your bedroom for a full week. Use an analogue alarm clock and don't look at your phone first thing in the morning or last thing before bed.

The Dinner Detox

Make every mealtime phone-free, even if you're eating alone. Focus on your food, your company, and the moment without screens competing for your attention.

The Notification Audit

Go into your phone settings and disable all non-essential notifications. Notice how your stress levels and concentration change over the week.

The Analogue Sunday

Spend one full Sunday without a smartphone. No social media, no apps, just offline living. Read, walk, cook, or connect with loved ones.
